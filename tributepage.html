Tony Allen
The man who created the beat 'Afrobeats'
Tony Allen at the Würzburg harbor summer on August 4th, 2010

Here is a time line of Tony Allen's Life:

  • 1940 - born in Lagos, Nigeria.
  • 1958 - begins playing drums while working as an engineer for a radio station.
  • 1964 - auditions for a jazz highlife band Fela Kuti was forming, upon invitation from Fela Kuti.
  • 1969 - serves as the musical director of Fela's band, Africa'70 during an educational trip to the United States.
  • 1970 - records more than 30 albums with Fela and Africa'70.
  • 1980 - forms his own group and records "No Discrimination" whilst performing in Lagos.
  • 1984 - emigrates to London.
  • 1985 - records with King Sunny Ade, Ray Lema and Manu Dibango, records "N.E.P.A".
  • 2002 - appears on the Red Hot Organization's compilation album "Red Hot and Riot" in tribute to Fela Kuti, appears alongside Res, Ray Lema, Baaba Maal, Positive Black Soul and Archie Shepp on a track titled "No Agreement".
  • 2003 - plays drums throughout the 2003 album love Trap by Susheela Raman and performs with her live.
  • 2004 - records with French electronic artist, Sébastien Tellier on the album Politics including the hit song "La Ritournelle" .
  • 2006 - records the album trippin with Amsterdam based New Cool Collective mixing his Afrobeat with jazz and did a series of concerts in the Netherlands, among them, the Lowlands Festival.
  • 2007 - plays drums on two tracks on the album 5:55 by Charlotte Gainsbourg: "5:55" and "Night Time Intermission", backed by French duo Air and Jarvis Cocker of Pulp.Alsp, makes appearance playing drums in the video for "Once Upon a Time" by French Duo Air in late 2007.
  • 2009 - releases his album Secret Agent in June by World Circuit .
  • 2012 - replaces Vladislav Delay as drummer for the Moritz Von Oswald Trio and appeared on their album Sounding Lines.
  • 2017 - releases "A Tribute to Art Blakey & the Jazz Messengers", a four-song EP on Blue Note Records featuring a reworked Afrobeat version of Art Blakey's "Moanin"
  • 2018 - records E.P Tomorrow Comes The Harvest with Techno DJ Jeff Mills.
  • 2019 - features in the documentary film Birth of Afrobeat a film by Opiyo Okeyo , a film about Allen's Life.
  • 2020 - died at the age of 79 in Paris, France.

without Tony Allen, there would be no Afrobeat.
